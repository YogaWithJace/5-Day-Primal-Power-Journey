Your Journey to Primal Power

Day 1: Ground Yourself

Discover foundational strength and stability with powerful martial arts stances and classic yoga poses. Learn to root yourself in the present moment.

Day 2: Unleash Your Flow

Embrace movement as medicine. You’ll learn a simple, short flow that combines yoga and martial arts to unlock your body's natural fluidity.

Day 3: Find Your Center

It's time for stillness. Use a guided meditation to calm your mind, find focus, and breathe through life's chaos.

Day 4: Cultivate Your Power

This is where the fire comes in. We’ll build on your strength with quick-fire workouts and power poses that will leave you feeling invincible.

Day 5: Integrate and Rise

Tie it all together. Recap your journey and discover how these daily habits lead to lasting change. It's the grand finale to a brand new you.

Meet the Guide

Jace, originally from South Africa, has been immersed in yoga and meditation for over 20 years. His journey began at a young age, inspired by his mother’s interest in holistic practices. From the very first moments on the mat, he discovered a sense of strength, clarity, and belonging — a deeper comfort in his own skin and a way to meet life’s stresses with presence rather than resistance. This search for clarity and self-understanding led him into a lifelong relationship with the practice, guiding him toward a deeper connection with himself and, in turn, with others.

His vast experience extends far beyond the mat, including martial arts like Capoeira, Muay Thai, Jeet Kune Do, and boxing, as well as the meditative practice of freediving. This background informs his unique teaching style, which spans a wide spectrum—from fiery, strength-building practices to slow, introspective meditation and Yoga Nidra. He is a 500-hour Registered Yoga Teacher (RYT) with Yoga Alliance and has spent the last eight years living and teaching in Thailand, leading advanced teacher trainings, workshops, and retreats internationally.

What makes Jace’s teaching unique is his belief that yoga doesn't ask us to choose between strength and softness, body and mind, or discipline and play—it invites us to embrace it all. His classes embody this philosophy, helping students cultivate strong, flexible, and mobile bodies while also awakening a deeper spiritual awareness.

“Ask yourself—is it helpful or harmful? Eliminate what doesn’t serve you, and embrace the truth that there is no failure, only progress and learning.”

- Jace
